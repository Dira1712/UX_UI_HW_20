CASE STUDY

Project Overview

Presentation Manor, situated in Toronto's Clairlea-
Birchmount neighborhood, serves as a senior
residence home providing a range of services tailored
to meet the diverse needs of its residents, ensuring a
fulfilling and enriching experience in their golden
years.
However, their website lacks vital information, leading
to hesitancy in bookings. To address this, stakeholders
are redesigning the website.
This case study explores the process of creating a user-
centric solution aligned with the residence's values.
Duration: 2 Weeks
Tools: Figma, Balsamiq,
Adobe Illustrator

Problem Statement

The current website for Presentation Manor Senior
Living faces several challenges that hinder user
experience and decision-making processes. These
include :

  • An outdated blog-style layout, which complicates
    navigation and reduces user-friendliness.

  • Lacks accessibility features, making it difficult for
    users with fine motor skills or poor vision to
    navigate effectively.

  • Incomplete information, challenges in comparing
    amenities and services, and a lack of transparency
    in pricing contribute to user frustration and hinder
    well-informed decision-making

Our Solution

Redesign the Presentation Manor website with the
focus on elevating websites' accessibility compliance
and enhancing content discovery through improved
organization, and user navigation.

  • Incorporate diverse and immersive videos
    showcasing social activities

  • Updated resident testimonials to effectively
    communicate the living experience

  • Virtual tours and a strong digital presence to
    provide comprehensive information and facilitate
    communication with users

  • Streamlining navigation structures

Design Process

User Research

Research Method:

  • Developed a research plan focusing on
    uncovering insights into UI issues and
    interaction challenges within the current
    design.

  • Gathered feedback from a diverse group
    of participants aged 40-75

  • Three broader categories of users:
    • prospective residents and families
    • current residents of Presentation Manor
    • key stakeholders like management and administrators

Stakeholder Quotes:

“We offer a lifestyle not just a home. What sets
us apart from other senior living communities is
our unique lifestyle and the sense of community
we offer and we want our website to be a
reflection of just that. ”


Stakeholder Interviews:

To grasp the project's scope, I initiated an interview with
the Director of Community Relations and Executive
Director of the residents. I categorized my findings into
three main areas: Outcomes, Needs, Desires, and
Constraints.

Outcomes:

  • Increase booked tours.
  • Establish a robust digital presence.
  • Foster greater family involvement and connectivity.
  • Find solution to better showcase the manor and its experience.

Needs:

  • Enhance user-friendliness for better navigation.
  • Design solutions catered to diverse age
    demographic.
  • Incorporate diverse and immersive videos to offer
    visitors a realistic experience of the community.

Desires:

  • Accessibility compliance to accommodate users with
    fine motor skills or poor vision.
  • Address the outdated blog- style website layout
  • Showcase state-of-art facilities , modern design
    elements that accurately represents the residence.

Constraints:

  • Low budget
  • A huge learning curve inherent in understanding
    CMS systems for continued maintenance and
    information updates.

User Interviews and Usability Insights:

To deepen our comprehension of the scope and devise
user-centric design solutions, we conducted a series of
six one-on-one usability tests and six one-on-one user
interviews specifically tailored to grasp the unique
needs, challenges, and barriers faced by prospective
residents and their families in their search for a
retirement home.

Outcomes:

  • Accessibility is crucial for the targeted demographic
  • Virtual tours and digital presence are highly valued by users.
  • Hassle- free scheduling of tours is important to the users.

Needs:

  • 3D walkthroughs and easy access to available floor plans.
  • Price transparency and room availability.
  • Access to comprehensive information about
    activities and amenities offered by the senior
    residence.
  • Lots of pictures and testimonials of current residents

UI Evaluation

  • The website did not follow a lot of UI
    heuristics and failed colour contrast ratio test.

  • Accessibility compliance issues, particularly
    with navigation text size, impacting users
    with fine motor skills or clicking difficulties.

  • Text links varied in design, making it
    difficult to distinguish between clickable
    links and regular text.

  • There is no availability of help and
    documentation and no socials present in the footer.

  • This underscored the necessity of
    integrating these heuristics to improve the
    design's efficiency and user-friendliness

  • The activity calendars and floor plans were
    available as downloadable PDF’s

Definition and Ideation:

Feature Prioritization:
Drawing from the feedback received, we've
pinpointed the key features that warrant prioritization as br we move forward with solution development

  • Simplify the website layout by reducing the number
    of tabs and displaying all relevant information on
    single page.

  • Integrate pricing details with suite option
    information such as availability , viewable image of
    floor plans instead of downloadable PDFs to enhance
    accessibility of information and simplify the decision
    making process

  • Redesign the website’s aesthetic s to create a warmer
    and more welcoming ambiance fostering a sense of
    connection and security for users

Paper Prototype:

On paper we redesigned and re-organized the user
flows before mapping out the iterations thoroughly
to understand the scope of the designs and the
concepts to propose to stakeholders.
Some of the major changes we proposed in the
redesign were the following:
  • Restructure the website's vital content onto a
    single page.

  • Utilize iconography to enhance visual clarity
    instead of listing common areas in the list format

  • Include floor plan previews instead of
    downloadable PDF’s and the option for a 3D
    walkthrough
  • Highlighted key accessibility parameters as a
    design solution to address accessibility
    parameters.

Style Guide:

    Senior Living signifies a significant step forward in

    residents.

  • The revamped website places a strong emphasis on

      features and enhance navigation.

Key takeaways and Next Steps:

Continuing the Presentation Manor Senior Living
website redesign project entails several key br future steps:
  • Accessibility Adjustments: Given additional time, our first course of action would be to develop wireframes specifically designed to integrate the proposed accessibility improvements. These wireframes would serve as a blueprint, outlining the structural layout and functionality of the website in a manner that ensures optimal accessibility for all users

  • Chat Feature: Given additional time, we would’ve liked to introduce an interactive chat feature that visitors could access to ask questions, receive directions, and enhance user engagement and support.

  • Enhancing Activity Calendar Functionality: Implementing features that allow users to interact seamlessly with and the option to view the calendar in different modes such as the traditional calendar mode or list view.
By implementing these future steps, Presentation Manor Senior Living can enhance the overall user experience, improve engagement, and ensure accessibility for all users, ultimately achieving a more effective and user-friendly website.
